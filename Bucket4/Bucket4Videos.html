Videos and Arithmetic Practice

Visual media can provide a great way to help children learn mathematics. Videos provide a fun context and can introduce friendly and memorable characters to help children feel comfortable with mathematics. It is important to keep in mind that children learn from screen time to whatever extent they are able to be engaged with the material. When parents watch along with their child and ask them questions or help them when they get stuck children learn the most. It is best to think of educational screen time much like you would think of shared storybook reading. Both you and your child should be engage with the material and you can help keep their attention on the mathematics by asking questions.

Storybook Alternatives

If you would prefer to focus on storybooks, there are several ways to incoporate math during book reading too!

What to look for: Although there are many number and math books available, almost any picture book can become a great resource for early math learning! The best books are books that you and your child enjoy reading together. Research has shown time and again that children learn the most when their caregivers ask questions as they read the book. By incorporating questions about math (e.g., "Who has the most apples?" or "Can you count the mice and tell me how many there are?"), you are helping your child learn foundational skills.

What to avoid:What to avoid: Lift-the-flap books can provide interactivity and surprises for book reading, but when trying to find a book for teaching specific concepts, these books can be distracting and make it harder for children to learn. Similarly, avoid books that draw attention away from mathematics or towards non-numerical features.

What to do on each page: After reading each page try to think of a question that compares quantities, asks your child to count, or requires them to predict what comes next.
