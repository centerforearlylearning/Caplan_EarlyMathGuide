About us

This guide for early mathematics learning is part of the Center for Early Learning at Purdue University. This project is part of the larger efforts of the Center to translate research into practice.

Dr. O’Rear is an educational researcher with more than a decade of experience developing educational interventions. Through this work he has helped to promote children's promote children's mathematical and literacy skills, with a focus on children from Head Start and Title I programs.

Alexa Ellis, PhD

Assistant Professor

Department of Human Development and Family Studies

Dr. Ellis is an expert in the area of children's early learning at school and at home. She has years of experience assessing children’s early mathematical development skills, focusing on caregiver (both parents and teachers) contributions to support early mathematical learning.

University of Alabama

David Purpura, PhD

Professor

Director of the Center for Early Learning

Department of Humand Development and Family Sciences

Purdue University

Dr. Purpura is a professor of human development and family sciences at Purdue and the co-director of the Center for Early Learning, a research, practice and policy center. He has extensive research and translational work on understanding and intervening on the development of young children’s mathematics skills including the development of brief and accurate screening tools, parent and teacher-led interventions, and engaging with caregivers to support children’s learning.
