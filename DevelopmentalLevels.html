Learn more about the development process and the developmental levels that are the focus in this guide.


The current guide is broken into four broad developmental milestones that are used to help pair specific types of activities with children's current skill level (detailed below). To identify where children fall along this developmental trajectory we used a data-driven approach to map out where in their development a child is likely to be. We then worked with over 50 experts in early education to identify the appropriateness of specific activities for promoting children across this developmental trajectory. Once we had identified a general set of activities we incorporated feedback from over 100 parents of three-to-five year old children to ensure we cover a wide variety of fun and useful activities! Below you can find the four broad points in development that we use in the guide to identify how each activitiy can best be tailored to a child's current skillset. You may find that the suggested activities are above or below your child's current interests or skill level. Feel free to explore other levels to find what works best for you!


Beginner Counters

Children at this point in development are beginning to understand how the numbers in the count follow a structured order, much like they learn the ABCs. The goal at this stage is to help children gain familiarity with important math concepts using different number games and songs.

Early Counters

Children at this point in development have an understanding of the initial structure of the count list, and are learning to extend this to higher numbers. This understanding is relatively rigid, with children knowing how to count starting from “one” but not yet understanding how counting can be applied in varied ways.

Consistent Counters

Children at this point in development are able to consistent count up to 10 and are learning how to extend this count into the teens. Additionally, children are developing a more flexible understanding of the count list, showing that they can count on from numbers within their counting list.

Advanced Counters

At this point in development, children may show a bigger interest in numbers, wanting to show off all the numerals they know! This understanding of how their number words connect to the numerals they provides children with a symbolic understanding of mathematics. Although children may know many different numerals, they will still struggle to name numbers they are unfamiliar with and will still need help linking these numerals to their relative set. For example, once children can identify 10 and 8, they may still need help to understand that 10 is more than 8. To help strengthen children's understanding of these mathematical symbols, regular exposure and use of numerals is necessary as well as regular comparison of different numerals. Fortunately, there are many fun activities, such as board and card games that allow children to show off their number naming and comparison skills!
